I work for a defense contractor developing algorithms, software, and firmware for GPS-denied navigation, wireless communications, and autonomous systems. I also develop lightweight motor technology for motion and propulsion systems demanding high torque density.

I have a Ph.D in Electrical & Computer Engineering from the University of California in Santa Barbara (UCSB), where I conducted research at the Center for Control, Dynamical Systems, and Computation (CCDC) under the advisement of Prof. Andrew Teel. I received the 2018-2019 award for Outstanding Teaching Assistant in Electrical Engineering at UCSB.

I received the M.S. degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of Nevada in Las Vegas (UNLV) in 2018, under the advisement of Prof. Pushkin Kachroo, and the B.S. degree in Electrical Engineering from UNLV in 2016, with a minor in Mathematics. In 2016, I received the Outstanding Undergraduate Research Award at UNLV.
